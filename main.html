Clara Zetkin

Here's a time line of Clara Zetkin's life:

  • 1857 - Was born in Wiederau, Kingdom of Saxony, German Confederation
  • 1878 - She joined the Socialist Workers' Party
  • 1880 - Due to the political climate in Germany, Zetkin went into exile in Switzerland and later in France
  • 1882 - Went into exile in Paris, where she studied to be a journalist and a translator
  • 1898 - Zetkin formed a friendship with the younger Rosa Luxemburg that lasted 20 years
  • 1916 - She was one of the co-founders of the Spartacist League and the Independent Social Democratic Party of Germany (USPD) which had split off in 1917 from its mother party, the SPD, in protest at its pro-war stance
  • 1920 - She interviewed Lenin on "The Women's Question"
  • 1922 - Was part of the prosecution team during the Trial of the Socialist Revolutionaries in Moscow, but at other times, she was critical of Moscow's influence over the German Communist Party, within which she was part of the right wing
  • 1932 - She returned to Berlin to preside over the opening of the newly elected Reichstag, as its oldest deputy
  • 1933 - Died at the age of 76 in Moscow
